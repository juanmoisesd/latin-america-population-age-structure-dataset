# A Harmonized Dataset of Population Age Structure in Latin America (1995–2030)

## Abstract

This paper presents a harmonized dataset describing the population age structure and key demographic indicators for 19 Latin American countries between 1995 and 2030. The dataset provides quinquennial observations of demographic structure disaggregated by sex and distributed across fifteen five-year age groups. In addition to age distribution, the dataset includes indicators such as total population size, fertility rates, life expectancy, infant mortality, migration balance, urbanization levels, population density, and dependency ratios. The resulting dataset contains 456 country–year–sex observations and 42 variables, enabling comparative analysis of demographic transition, population aging, and demographic dividend dynamics across the region. The dataset has been compiled from international statistical sources and harmonized to ensure cross-country comparability. It provides a structured resource for research in demography, development economics, population studies, and public policy planning.

---

# 1. Background and Rationale

Demographic change represents one of the most important structural transformations affecting societies worldwide. Over the past decades, Latin America has experienced profound demographic transitions characterized by declining fertility rates, increasing life expectancy, changes in migration patterns, and progressive population aging.

These demographic dynamics have important implications for labor markets, economic development, health systems, social protection, and public policy design. In particular, the region has moved through different phases of the demographic transition, including periods associated with the demographic dividend and emerging challenges linked to population aging.

Although demographic information is widely available through international organizations and national statistical offices, researchers frequently face difficulties when attempting to conduct comparative analyses across countries and time periods. Differences in data formats, variable definitions, age group classifications, and temporal coverage often limit the comparability of demographic datasets.

To address these limitations, this project compiles and harmonizes demographic data from multiple international sources in order to provide a consistent dataset describing the population age structure of Latin American countries between 1995 and 2030.

The resulting dataset allows researchers to examine regional demographic trends and conduct comparative analyses across countries using a unified structure.

# 2. Methods

## 2.1 Data Sources

The dataset was constructed by compiling demographic information from several international and regional statistical sources, including:

- Economic Commission for Latin America and the Caribbean (CEPAL)
- World Bank demographic databases
- BBVA Research demographic reports
- National statistical institutes of Latin American countries
- Additional demographic estimates and projections from international datasets

These sources provide population estimates and projections for multiple demographic indicators relevant to population structure and demographic change.

## 2.2 Data Harmonization

Because the original sources present demographic information using different formats and classification schemes, the data were harmonized to ensure comparability across countries and time periods.

The harmonization process involved:

- Standardizing age classifications into **quinquennial age groups**
- Aligning temporal observations to **quinquennial intervals**
- Harmonizing demographic indicators across sources
- Ensuring consistency between population totals and age group distributions
- Converting absolute population values to comparable units where necessary

Age structure was standardized into fifteen five-year age groups ranging from **0–4 years to 70 years and older**.

## 2.3 Unit of Observation

Each observation in the dataset corresponds to a unique combination of:

Country × Year × Sex

Sex categories included in the dataset are:

- H — Men  
- M — Women  
- TOTAL — Total population

This structure allows researchers to analyze demographic patterns both at the aggregate population level and by sex.

# 3. Data Records

The dataset includes demographic information for **19 Latin American countries**.

## 3.1 Temporal Coverage

The dataset covers the period:

**1995–2030**

Observations are provided at **quinquennial intervals**:

1995  
2000  
2005  
2010  
2015  
2020  
2025  
2030

## 3.2 Dataset Size

The dataset contains:

- **19 countries**
- **8 time points**
- **3 sex categories**

Total observations:

**456 records**

The dataset includes **42 variables**, covering demographic indicators and population structure measures.

## 3.3 Variables

The dataset includes three main groups of variables.

### Identification variables

- Country  
- Year  
- Sex

### Demographic indicators

- Total population (millions)
- Urban population percentage
- Total fertility rate
- Life expectancy
- Infant mortality
- Net migration
- Population density
- Dependency ratio

### Age structure variables

Population age structure is represented through fifteen quinquennial age groups.

Two complementary representations are provided:

1. Percentage distribution of population by age group  
2. Absolute population counts (in thousands) for each age group

Providing both measures allows researchers to analyze demographic structure in relative and absolute terms.

# 4. Technical Validation

Several validation procedures were applied to ensure the internal consistency and reliability of the dataset.

These procedures included:

- verification that percentage distributions across age groups sum to 100
- consistency checks between population totals and age group population counts
- cross-validation of demographic indicators with original statistical sources
- verification of temporal consistency in demographic trends

These checks ensure that the dataset maintains internal coherence and is suitable for comparative demographic analysis.
