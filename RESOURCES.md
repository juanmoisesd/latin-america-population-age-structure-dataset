# Additional Resources and Data Sources

To complement the data provided in this repository, the following international and regional sources are recommended:

## 1. Regional Organizations
- **CEPALSTAT (ECLAC)**: Main source for socioeconomic and demographic statistics in Latin America.
  - [statistics.cepal.org](https://statistics.cepal.org/)
- **CELADE**: Population Division of ECLAC, specializing in demographic analysis and census data.
  - [cepal.org/celade](https://www.cepal.org/es/areas-de-trabajo/poblacion-y-desarrollo)

## 2. International Databases
- **World Bank Open Data**: Global demographic indicators, fertility, and life expectancy.
  - [data.worldbank.org](https://data.worldbank.org/)
- **United Nations Population Division (WPP)**: World Population Prospects - the most authoritative source for global projections.
  - [population.un.org](https://population.un.org/wpp/)
- **Our World in Data**: Excellent visualizations and historical contexts for demographic transitions.
  - [ourworldindata.org/population-growth](https://ourworldindata.org/population-growth)

## 3. Tools for Analysis
- **Frictionless Data**: Documentation and tools for managing Data Packages.
  - [frictionlessdata.io](https://frictionlessdata.io/)
- **Pandas (Python)**: The industry standard for data manipulation in demographic research.
  - [pandas.pydata.org](https://pandas.pydata.org/)
